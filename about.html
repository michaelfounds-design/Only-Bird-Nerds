About Only Bird Nerds

OnlyBirdNerds started with a simple frustration: birding is an experience, but listing turns it into a spreadsheet. For me, birding is meditative. It's a way to focus, slow down, and spend time outside. The data I collect should be worth something more than rows.

I wanted a way to revisit past trips that wasn't a folder of blurry phone pics or a half-colored map of where I've been. So I built an interactive map of my own eBird data, and I wanted to share it!

From there I added features to let you explore your own sightings, replay your best days, and plan your next quest. Why turn it into a game with quests? Because your life list shouldn't be the only thing that counts. There should be a way to value the journey, the local patch you know by heart, the rainy morning that turned up one good bird, even if you'll never see 8,000 species.

What's next

There's a lot of room to make this into a really fun way to learn and explore. The bigger idea I keep coming back to: birders generate an enormous amount of spatial data, and there's real potential in connecting it to conservation and education. Will OnlyBirdNerds replace Instagram as the leading birding social platform? We'll have to see.
